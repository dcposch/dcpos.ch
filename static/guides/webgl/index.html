✨ webgl ✨

webgl is the web api for 3d rendering.

the zen of webgl is simple and beautiful. you describe your world as data, giant tables of numbers. x, y, and z coordinates. color. shininess. the angle of each blade of grass.

then, you define pure functions called shaders that map those numbers first to vertices, then to pixels.

shaders are written in glsl, a dialect of c. it's compiled on the fly, by the browser.

here in webgl, we'll be writing our shaders as tiny programs with a main() method, just as you're used to. but this main() will be called several times per pixel, for millions of pixels, 60 times a second!

the highly optimized, massively parallel hardware in every modern computer and phone--the gpu, or graphics processing unit--loads the tables of data, called buffers, then runs your shaders. first, the vertex shaders run once for each vertex in the scene. three vertices form a triangle.

the hardware efficiently rasterizes the triangles--figuring out which pixels each triangle touches. then, for each pixel x each triangle, your fragment shader produces a color. if multiple triangles touch a single pixel, you get many outputs. the hardware can use transparency to combine those fragments into the final pixel. this allows you to make things see-through.
